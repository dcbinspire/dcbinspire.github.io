Russia in Revolution (1914 - 24)






The March Revolution


1.     Weakness of Tsar Nicholas II

a.     Weak but obstinate

b.     Refused to work with democratic dumas

c.     Violence toward uprisings

                                               i.     Bloody Sunday

                                              ii.     Lena Goldfield Massacre -170 killed

d.     Lack of interest in the world around him

                                               i.     Did not visit factories and villages

                                              ii.     Trusted people who wanted to protect him

                                             iii.     Trusted wrong people - Rasputin

e.     Left for the battle front, Rasputin in charge of the government

2.     Situation in Russia

a.     Tried to industrialize in a short period of time

                                               i.     Food prices went up, wages went down

                                              ii.     Working longer time for lower wages

b.     Reform in countryside

                                               i.     Stolypin - peasants buying land  

                                              ii.     However, 15% successful

                                             iii.     Poor peasants forced to sell their lands, move into the city

                                            iv.     Four million peasants encouraged settling along the Trans-Siberian Railway. However, richer ones already took over

                                             v.     Stolypin's Necktie

c.     Bad Winter 1916 ~ 1917

                                               i.     Trans-Siberian railway froze

d.     Rasputin in charge after Tsar left, economic and social problems unsolved, bad political decisions

3.     WWI

a.     Bad leadership->affected the front

                                               i.     1,000,000 casualties by September 1914

                                              ii.     Generals and officers were Tsar's friend

1.     They were often incompetent

                                             iii.     Decreasing Morale 25% deserters

b.     Supplies

                                               i.     Lack of food, medical support, supplies and armaments

                                              ii.     Peasants and workers sent to war, no one to work

c.     Treatment

                                               i.     Little respect

                                              ii.     No medical treatment-> priest 'blessing' them

The November Revolution


1.     Strength of the Bolshevik leaders

a.     Lenin

                                               i.     Persuasive

                                              ii.     April thesis = Slogan - 'Bread, Peace, Land'

                                             iii.     He knew it was the right time - persuaded K and Z

1.     Argued all night on 25th of October

b.     Trotsky

                                               i.     Persuaded Lenin to wait until the congress of all-Soviet

                                              ii.     Rebuked the socialist revolutionaries, left the soviet

2.     Weakness of Provisional government

a.     War

                                               i.     Did not end the war

                                              ii.     High prices and shortage of food

                                             iii.     October 1917, the average bread ration for a worker = 110g

b.     Land

                                               i.     Didn't give the land back to peasants, peasants went mad

                                              ii.     237 land seizures by june 1917

                                             iii.     Unpopular, weak

3.     Kerensky

a.     Kornilov affair

b.     Kerensky gave weapons to the Red guards for help

c.     Bolsheviks, Gained 51% of the votes in the Soviet in October 1917

The Civil War (The War of Communism)


1.     Leadership of Trotsky

a.     Organized army

                                               i.     Reorganized the army. Strict hierarchical lines

                                              ii.     Brought back 50,000 experienced imperial officers

                                             iii.     Deserters to be shot

                                            iv.     Political commissars for all officers

b.     Trotsky's mobile train

                                               i.     Raised people's morale

2.     Geographical advantages

a.     Central area of Western Russia, Petrograd and Moscow

b.     Controlled the railway

                                               i.     Faster transport of soldiers and resources

                                              ii.     Plenty of munitions and war supplies

c.     Controlled the factories

d.     Central area - heavily populated

                                               i.     Conscription - Larger armies

3.     Weaknesses of the Whites

a.     No unity

                                               i.     Made of different groups of people with different aims

1.     Tsarist + Socialist + Liberals

                                              ii.     Can't trust each other + Can't work together

                                             iii.     White generals suspicious of Kolchak's motives

b.     No leadership

                                               i.     Cruel, doing drugs

                                              ii.     Indiscipline + Corruption

1.     Denikin: 'I can do nothing with my army.

Bolshevik Consolidation of Power


1.     NEP

a.     Dealt with crisis facing the peasants and workers

b.    Abolished grain requisitioning

                                               i.     Opportunity to increase in wealth

c.     Private trade and small business (profit)

                                               i.     Workers became happier and more willing to produce

d.    1923 - cereal production 23% compared to 1920

e.     Factory production increased by 200%

f.      1926 production level = 1913 level

g.    Encouraged foreign trade

2.     Censorship

a.     'agitprop' trains to promote message of revolution

b.    Pamphlets and posters to encourage communist ideas

c.     Invested a lot in education

                                               i.     Komsomols set up to encouragege communist ideas

d.    Censorship of books and writings - Glavlit

3.     Terror and Violence

a.     Cheka renamed GPU

                                               i.     Nepmen periodically harassed by checka, arrested

b.    Communist party mounted extreme attack on organized religion as threat to power

                                               i.     1921, militant godless, Challenging church

c.     1922: communist party ordered stripping of the church

                                               i.     Imprisonment of priests

d.    Caused fear and people scared to fight back

e.     Peasant revolt, Tambov region destroyed

Collectivization (1929)


Why?

1.     Economic reasons

a.     Strong base for rapid industrialization

b.     War needed money

c.     Grains export = money for weapons and to feed workers

                                               i.     Grain hoarding -> Collectivization, grain procurement

                                              ii.     Easy to procure  - Machine tractor station

2.     Ideological reasons

a.     Collectivization = peasants working together = communism

b.     Peasants using machinery = fewer peasants needed = more workers 17mil

c.     Marxist theory - most should be workers

d.     Rural agro-towns

                                               i.     Access to government run schools, canteens and theatres

 

Effects

 

1.     Negative: 25thousanders -> Dekulakization causing oppositions + Famine 1932~1934 Famine

a.     Riots and armed resistance

                                               i.     Burned crops, tools and houses

                                              ii.     Slaughter and eat and sell animals -67mil in 1928 to 36mil in 1934

b.     13mil death

                                               i.     Kulaks removed, no more hard working farmers

                                              ii.     No international aid sent

                                             iii.     Same amount of grain procurement

2.     Positive

a.     90% collectivized by end of 1929

b.     Grain production increased

                                               i.     In 1937, back to 1913 level, 93mil tons

c.     Dekulakization = eradicated the possible future enemy for socialism

d.     17million peasants left the countryside, more workers

e.     Governmental control of peasants - MTS

Industrialization

1)    Reasons

a.     Marxist theory = workers (1930 only 20%)

                                               i.     Many regions of the USSR were backwards

                                             ii.     To modernize Soviet industry

b.    Catch up with the west

                                               i.     Propaganda for communism and Stalin

                                             ii.     Roads were unmade, most people lived in villages and had no electricity

c.     Stalin believed the overtaking of countries

                                               i.     Thought Germany would invade and needed to catch up

                                             ii.     War economy

2)    Features

a.     Planned economy

                                               i.     Five year plans High goals, 75million tons of coals by 1932

b.     Specialists from other countries

                                               i.     The Dnieper dam project carried out with Americans

                                              ii.     Ford - 140,000cars in 1932

c.     Single managers

                                               i.     Easy to blame

                                              ii.     Better awards if well done

d.     Spectacular achievements

                                               i.     Dnieper Damn

                                              ii.     Moscow-Volga canal

                                             iii.     Moscow Metro

                                            iv.     Magnitogorsk

e.     Workers stay motivated because

                                               i.     A better society, young men wanted to improve their country

                                              ii.     Propaganda, posters

                                             iii.     Awards, piece work, higher awards if worked harder

                                            iv.     Punishments for bad workers

                                             v.     Stakhanovite movement in 1935 for motivation

3)    Five year plans

a.     Positive

                                               i.     Industrialized the country of Russia and was able to catch up with the west  

                                             ii.     USSR was self-sufficient by 1937, heavy industry: power plants, Increased communications and the chemical industry grew

                                            iii.     Defense grew and it was able to be defend WW2

                                            iv.     No unemployment; increase in demand for women workers; less misuse of children in industrialization

                                             v.     First 2 five year plans concentrated on the steel industry (coal, oil, steel, industry)

                                            vi.     STATS (1921 ~ 1940)

1.     Coal:  9 mils to 166 mil tons

2.     Steel: 0.2 mill to 18 mil tons

 

b.     Negative

                                               i.     Quality was poor

                                             ii.     The target was only the number of goods (Belomar Canal)

                                            iii.     Terrible human costs: Late for work = arrest able offence

1.     Many were arrested for when a machine was broken

2.     Secret police

3.     Slave labour (3mil prisoners worked on Baltic sea Canal were kulaks)

4.     Labour camps

5.     Few consumer goods

6.     Poor housing

7.     Wages fell

8.     No human rights

                                            iv.     Political prisoners were used as slaves.

                                             v.     Poorly organized- inefficient and duplication of effort

Development of dictatorship: Germany (1918 - 1945)



Increased opposition 1939~


1.     Losing the war

a.     Army swore an oath of loyalty to Hitler

                                               i.     Helped him maintain power

b.     1942, bad for the army

                                               i.     Invasion of USSR - Battle of Stalingrad

                                              ii.     Surrendered January 1943 200,000 German troops killed since November

c.     Lost land

                                               i.     1944 - Red Army invades Poland

d.     Army leaders feared their lives

                                               i.     Operation Valkyrie 1944

1.     Although failed but proved

2.     Situations for civilians worsened

a.     Women

                                               i.     Told to have more babies during the war for more soldiers

                                              ii.     Also forced to work due to shortages of labor (3million called upon, 1 million went to work)

b.     Rationing

                                               i.     1943: Hitler announced Total war - Anything irrelevant = eliminated

                                              ii.     August 1943- Clothes rationing stopped

                                             iii.     Monotonous diet with bread

c.     Allies bombing

                                               i.     First air raid in Berlin in August 1940

                                              ii.     Frequent bombing by 1942

                                             iii.     1945, 150,000 people killed in Dresden + 70% of the city destroyed in two nights

3.     Young People

a.     Nazi policies directing young Germans for war

b.     Best leaders of the Hitler Youth left for the army

                                               i.     Teenager leaders = ineffective

c.     Hitler Youth became less fun

                                               i.     Less camping and more actual combat training

                                              ii.     Less attractive

                                             iii.     Many turned away and formed gangs such as White Roses

Women


1.     Belief of the Nazis

a.     Although had variety of jobs = 100,000 teachers, 13,000 musicians and moreÉ Women's role = work at home

b.     Main role of Nazis = bear kids

                                               i.     Heavy hipped + Athletic + no make up

                                              ii.     Kids for future Nazis

c.     Propaganda   

                                               i.     Social loans for women not to work

                                              ii.     800,000 took part

                                             iii.     Medals for babies

d.     15% of teachers + all women doctors sacked 1934~1935

2.     Preparation of war

a.     Men=army, women = jobs

                                               i.     Compulsory 'duty year' in 1937

                                              ii.     1939 = Fewer women working than during Weimar Germany before the Great Depression

b.     Too much pressure, babies + jobs

                                               i.     Low wages + Poor conditions

c.     High birthrate wanted

                                               i.     Under the Nazis for 6 years = 40% birthrate increase

d.     Lebensborn = Program

                                               i.     Donating a baby to the Furher by becoming pregnant by 'racially pure' SS men

3.     During the war

a.     Many casualties during war for example 1942 invasion of USSR = 200,000 Germans

b.     Needed more babies and armament

                                               i.     Women did not want to bear babies during a war

                                              ii.     Rationing, bombing and absence of husband = bad condition

c.     Mobilization of women in 1943

                                               i.     3 mil women between 17 to 45 were called to work

                                              ii.     1 mil accepted

                                             iii.     Many acted ill or got pregnant on purpose



Unemployment


1.     The New Plan (1934), exporting more than importing. Reduce unemployment + building armament industries

a.     Dr. Hjalmar Schacht

                                               i.     March 1933 = appointed president of Reichsbank

                                              ii.     1934 = Economic minister

                                             iii.     Expansionary fiscal policy to boost the economy

                                            iv.     Regulated imports to make Germany an Autarky

b.     Trade agreements with foreign countries

                                               i.     Raw materials with Hungary in 1934

c.     Unemployment reduced with rising demand and production level

                                               i.     Conscription into the army

                                              ii.     Replacing Jewish and political opponents' jobs for unemployed Germans

2.     Statistical error

a.     Women and Jews pressured out of employment

b.     Some group workers no longer eligible to register for unemployment (agricultural workers)

c.     The Youth Service took off young people off the unemployment register (400,000 in 1934)

d.     Conscription removed men for military service for 2 years

                                               i.     Armed forces grew up to 1.4mil in 1939

3.     Public work schemes

a.     Paid private firms to build hospitals and schools to create jobs

b.     Massive road building projects to make 7000km of autobahns

c.     Building of homes and motorways

d.     Every worker belonged to German Labor Front

                                               i.     Workbook to record hours

Conflict, crisis and change: China (c. 1911 - c. 1989)


Boxer Rebellion 1900


1.     Course: Boxers blamed the 'Foreign devils'

a.     At first - also blamed the Manchus, but Empress Dowager Cixi gave them support - good

b.     Killed captured Christians and Europeans - Europeans sent an armed force to Beijing - crushed by a Chinese army

c.     Burned the French Cathedral and trapped nearly 1000 foreigners

 

2.     Effects: Angry Europeans - Six-nation force invaded China - captured and looted

a.     Fine of 67 million pounds + executed Boxers

b.     Harsh method to suppress the Boxers made them even angrier

c.     Also showed how weak the Manchus were

 

1911 Revolution – Wuchang


1.     Course:

a.     Sichuan - soldiers fired at the demonstrators - killed many

                                               i.     Tongmenhui went to Sichuan and spread the rebellion

b.     Double Tenth - Soldiers of the New Army in Wuchang joined

c.     From Wuchang - spread to central and southern China

                                               i.     End of November - 15/18 provinces had joined the revolution

d.     Yuan Shikai called back to crush rebels - made prime minister

e.     Quickly recaptured Wuchang, but made a deal with the rebels

                                               i.     Yuan = President

2.     Effects:

a.     Yuan Shikai became the president in 1912 with his military superiority

b.     Sun gave up due to fear of civil war

c.     Republic + future dictatorship (1912~1916)
