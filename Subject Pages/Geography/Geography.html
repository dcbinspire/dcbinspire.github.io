Population and Settlement



1.1 Population Dynamics

REQUIRED CASE STUDIES:
  • A country which is over-populated
  • A country which is under-populated
  • A country with a high rate of natural population growth
  • A country with a low rate of population growth (or population decline)

    • CASE STUDY: BANGLADESH

  • A country that is over-populated
  • A country with a high rate of natural growth
  • A densely populated country


    • Population Density: ~1298 per km2
    Population Growth Rate: +1.6%
    Birth rate: 21.14 per 1000
    Death rate: 5.61 per 1000

    Contraceptive prevalence: 61.2%
    Average age at first birth: 18.1

    Physicians density: 0.36 per 1000
    Hospital beds density: 0.6 per 1000

    Terrain:
    - Mostly flat alluvial plain
    - Hilly in SE

    Land Use:
    - Agricultural : 70.1%
    - Forest : 11.1%
    - Other : 18.8%

    Most populated:
    - Area around the Ganges river
    - 200-600mm of rainfall

    Unemployment: 8.7%


    CASE STUDY: AUSTRALIA

  • A sparsely populated country
  • A country that is underpopulated


    • Population Density: ~3 per km2
    Population Growth Rate: +1.03%
    Birth Rate: 12.1 per 1000
    Death Rate: 7.3 per 1000

    Contraceptive prevalence: 72.3%
    Average age at first birth: 30.5

    Physicians density: 3.37 per 1000
    Hospital beds density: 3.9 per 1000
    21 years of free education
    11.6 million employed in the workforce

    Terrain: mostly low plateau with deserts; fertile plain in SE

    Land Use: - Agricultural : 53.4%
    - Forest : 19.3%
    - Other : 27.3%

    Most populated: - 300-400mm of rainfall
    - 21-24˚C average daily maximum temperature
    - 0-300m above sea level

    CASE STUDY: ITALY

  • A country with a high dependent population
  • A country with a low rate of population growth


    • 8 / 10 Italians < 30 y/o live with their parents
    Average age for moving out: 36
    Average wage for Italians 25-30 = half of their counter-parts in England
    40% that leave their parents’ home must return
    Workers to pensioners’ ratio = 0.7
    2008: 21% of Italy’s GNP spent on caring for people > 65 y/o

    Population Growth Rate: 0.23%
    Birth Rate: 8.7 per 1000
    Death Rate: 10.3 per 1000
    Percentage population >65: 23.7%
    Total Dependency Ratio: 56.5%

    Mother’s mean age at first birth: 30.3
    Life expectancy at birth: 82.2
    Total fertility rate: 1.43 per woman


    1.2 Migration

    REQUIRED CASE STUDIES:
  • An international migration

    • CASE STUDY: International Migration from Mexico -> USA

  • An international migration


    • Crime rates (push factor): - 81% of Mexicans describe crime as a problem in Mexico
    - Homicide rates: 10-14 per 100,000 in Mexico ; lower in USA
    - Last 5 years: 47,500 killed in drug-related crimes

    Unemployment (push factor): - 2.2 (2002) -> 5.37 (2010)
    - 47% live below the poverty line

    Standards of living (both push/pull factor):
    - Mexico: 1 doctor per 1800
    - USA: 1 doctor per 400 - Life expectancy in Mexico = 72; USA = 76 - Adult literacy rate: Mexico = 55%; USA = 99%
